Paul Hayne

Assistant Professor
Astrophysical & Planetary Sciences Department
University of Colorado, Boulder

My research focuses on terrestrial planets and moons. I develop models that simulate planetary physics, in order to interpret observations from spacecraft and ground-based telescopes. I am especially interested in snow and ice, and the interactions of polar ice caps with planetary atmospheres.

Many dramatic phenomena in our Solar System arise from the dynamic behavior of volatiles. The coldest, frozen regions (cryospheres) of planetary bodies also record their climate histories in icy layers, as on Earth. The Exploration of Planetary Ices and Cryospheres (EPIC) group uses robotic spacecraft investigate the atmospheres, surfaces, and interiors of planets. New discoveries also drive advances in instrumentation and other technology, allowing us to probe planetary bodies in new and exciting ways. I am actively involved in these instrument and mission development efforts. I am also current member of several NASA missions, including Europa Clipper, Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter, Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter, and Lunar Flashlight.

Before joining CU in January of 2018, I was a postdoctoral scholar at Caltech, and then a staff scientist at NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory. I am actively looking for students and postdoctoral researchers to join my group at the University of Colorado's Laboratory for Atmospheric and Space Physics (LASP).
